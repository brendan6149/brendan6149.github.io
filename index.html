My Journey

Sea Glass

I've been collecting sea glass for a number of years now. My favourite beaches are on the north coast of Co Antrim and the beaches of south Co Down. Recently I found a rare sea glass marble.

These were originally used in bottles of carbonated drinks from the 1870s to the 1930s. The marble acted as a stopper to keep the fizz in the bottle. Unsurprisingly, children would break the bottle for the marble inside.

Sea Pottery

Again found mostly on the eastern coast of Northern Ireland, I've found many pieces with interesting and intriguing designs and markings. The piece below from a Keilller & Sons marmalade jar, which could be from the 1860s, though given its condition is probably somewhat later than that.

This is one of a number of pieces I've been able to identify.

Driftwood Creations

What started as collecting sticks for the fire has now evolved into using small and unusual pieces to create thoughtful and wistful pieces. The driftwood not cut or manipulated. The small houses and cottages are made from driftwood also and cut where necessary for the desired effect.
